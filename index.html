The Hand Behind the Colour

With 30+ years of craft refined across some of California's most distinguished salons, Persona was born from a singular conviction — that exceptional colour is not a service, but a collaboration. Each appointment is a careful study of light, tone, texture, and you. Over the course of that career, a calling to teach emerged — leading to years spent educating fellow colourists through Toni & Guy, L'Oréal, and Aveda, three of the industry's most exacting institutions. This atelier brings that same depth of knowledge directly to the Mendocino Coast.

Now rooted in Fort Bragg, California, Persona offers the intimacy of an independent studio with the precision of a career built at the highest level of the craft.

The pursuit of mastery has never been a destination — it is a practice. A deep commitment to continued learning has shaped every decade of this career, from seeking out the industry's most rigorous education to staying genuinely curious about what comes next. Teaching and learning have always been two sides of the same conversation, and that conversation never ends.

An Eye for Light

Where Light Lives

Before the first foil is placed, there is observation. The way morning light falls through a north-facing window. The way artificial light flattens what natural light reveals. This attunement to light is the signature that has defined this practice for thirty-seven years.

Colour that only performs under a salon's LED deserves more. Every formulation here is considered against the full spectrum of light your life actually moves through — sun, shadow, candlelight, cloud.

Natural Light
The Mendocino Coast offers a quality of diffused coastal light that is among the most honest in the world. Colour is read here first.
Artificial Light
Warm incandescent, cold LED, candlelight — each shifts tone. The formulation must hold beauty across all of them.
The Shift
Great colour is alive. It changes with the hour, the season, the room. That movement is not a flaw — it is the whole point.

Colour Aftercare · Protecting Your Investment
Cool water onlyHot water lifts the cuticle and fades pigment. Rinse with cool or lukewarm water to keep colour sealed and shining longer.
Wash less oftenColour-treated hair retains vibrancy when washed 2–3 times per week at most. Dry shampoo is your friend between appointments.
Sulphate-free productsUse a sulphate-free, colour-safe shampoo and a moisturising conditioner. Davines is recommended and available for purchase.
Heat protectionAlways apply a heat-protectant before blow-drying or styling. High heat is the fastest route to dull, porous colour.
Schedule your returnMost colour services last 6–10 weeks. Booking before you leave ensures you keep the look that was crafted for you.

Beyond the Atelier
Disjecta Membra
It began as a ritual of sorts developed over the last two years. When I catch a glimpse of a reflection — a light refraction, or the mark a graffiti buff had rendered in such a way that brings to mind thoughts of Rothko — something in me stops. The players, trash really — detritus and throwaways — are all found in and around the location where this perceived resonance occurs. They seemingly raise up as if to say "here I am."
I become hyper aware in the moment — alone, my attention scanning the environment with music playing in my ears. I compose and assemble the found objects until I feel a bell ring. It's done. I then photograph my unique composition. Assembling these ephemeral works — cairns, as I have come to call them — seems to bring about a cathartic state, as if I've balanced an energy distortion. At times I leave them. Mostly I dismantle them. They've served a purpose.
